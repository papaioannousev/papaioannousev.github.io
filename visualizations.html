Data-driven visualization

Here is a data-driven visualization regarding the consumption of water worldwide. The primary reason I like this visualization is that it presents information in a straight-forward and engaging way, while it gives the user a high-level overview of the data.

It employs multiple visuals (maps, icons) and different coloring to convey information (e.g. droplet color, region color).The colour palette featuring shades of blue is a symbolic choice since it matches the colour of the water, while is also a colorblind-friendly option.

By providing multiple views of the information, becomes easier to make comparisons (e.g. between coutries or continents) and identify patterns (e.g. decreased water consumption in poorer regions such as Africa and Latin America). In this manner, the visualization enables storytelling.

Finally, it highlights significant parts that leave a lasting impact to the readers, such as the fact that 1 in 8 people do not have access to clean water. It also backs the information displayed with the corresponding quantities without overwhelming the user by spacing out numbers with visual elements and texts.
